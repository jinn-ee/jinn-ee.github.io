Conversion Rate Optimization (CRO)

The power of conversion optimization - How to maximize ROI and boost bottom line.

JINN.ee
Last updated on 30 March 2023 10 min read

Some synonyms of Conversion Optimization are:

  • Conversion Rate Optimization (CRO)
  • Conversion Improvement
  • Conversion Maximization
  • Conversion Enhancement
  • Conversion Boosting

The benefits that clients typically expect from a conversion optimization service

  1. Increased Conversion Rates: Clients want their website visitors to take action and become customers. Conversion optimization aims to improve the user experience and increase the percentage of visitors who convert into customers.
  2. Improved User Experience: Clients want their website to be user-friendly, easy to navigate, and engaging. Conversion optimization helps to identify and remove barriers that prevent visitors from engaging with the website and taking action.
  3. Better Return on Investment (ROI): Clients want to maximize the return on their investment in digital marketing. Conversion optimization helps to increase the effectiveness of digital marketing campaigns by improving the conversion rates of the website.
  4. Enhanced Brand Image: Clients want their brand to be seen as professional, trustworthy, and authoritative. A well-designed and optimized website can help to enhance the brand image and improve the overall perception of the business.
  5. Competitive Advantage: Clients want to stay ahead of their competitors and stand out in their industry. Conversion optimization can help to differentiate the business and create a competitive advantage by providing a better user experience and higher conversion rates.

By committing to delivering these benefits through your conversion optimization service, you can help your clients to achieve their goals and grow their business.

To deliver on these benefits, you may consider implementing strategies such as A/B testing, user testing, website design improvements, copywriting improvements, and more. By analyzing user behavior and identifying areas for improvement, you can create a tailored plan to help your clients achieve their desired outcomes.

Remember to set clear expectations with your clients and communicate the progress of your work regularly. This can help to build trust and ensure that your clients are satisfied with your services. By consistently delivering on the promised benefits, you can establish yourself as a trusted and reliable provider of conversion optimization services.

To set expectations properly and deliver on the benefits of conversion optimization, you should ask your client the following questions

  1. What are your current conversion rates?
  2. What is your desired conversion rate?
  3. Who is your target audience?
  4. What are your main conversion goals (e.g., form submissions, product purchases, etc.)?
  5. What metrics are you using to track conversions (e.g., Google Analytics, heat maps, etc.)?
  6. What is your budget for conversion optimization services?
  7. Have you done any conversion optimization testing before? If so, what were the results?
  8. Are there any specific pain points or issues that you have identified with your current conversion process?
  9. How will you measure the success of the conversion optimization campaign?
  10. How involved do you want to be in the conversion optimization process (e.g., do you want to be kept informed of every change or only receive updates periodically)?

By asking these questions, you can gain a better understanding of your client’s business, goals, and pain points. This information can then be used to create a customized conversion optimization plan that addresses their specific needs and helps them achieve the desired benefits.

To help your client achieve the benefits of conversion optimization, you can offer the following concrete deliverables

  1. Conversion rate audit: Start with a detailed analysis of your client’s website to identify areas for improvement and potential barriers to conversion. This will help to establish a baseline and provide insights that can be used to inform the other deliverables.
  2. User experience (UX) analysis: Once you have identified areas for improvement from the conversion rate audit, perform a UX analysis to identify specific elements that need to be improved to enhance the user experience.
  3. Conversion funnel analysis: Analyze the user journey from entry point to conversion to identify areas where users are dropping off or getting stuck. This will help to optimize the conversion funnel and improve conversion rates.
  4. Landing page optimization: Use the insights gained from the conversion rate and UX analyses to create and test landing pages with clear calls to action and messaging that resonates with the target audience.
  5. A/B testing plan: Once the landing pages have been optimized, create an A/B testing plan to determine which variations of web pages, headlines, images, and other elements produce the best conversion rates.
  6. Personalization strategy: Once the core elements of the website have been optimized, create a personalization strategy to provide a more customized experience for visitors based on their behavior, demographics, and other data points.
  7. Retargeting campaigns: Finally, create retargeting campaigns to reach users who have previously visited the website and encourage them to convert.

By following this order, you can ensure that each deliverable builds upon the insights gained from the previous one, and that there are no conflicting dependencies.

Conversion rate audit

To create a conversion rate audit, you would need to gather information about the client’s website and their goals. Here are some questions that can help you get started:

  1. What is the primary goal of your website?
  2. What specific actions do you want visitors to take on your website?
  3. What is your current conversion rate?
  4. How do you currently measure conversions?
  5. Who is your target audience, and what are their needs and pain points?
  6. What is your unique selling proposition?
  7. What is your current traffic volume and sources?
  8. What is your website’s bounce rate, and which pages have the highest bounce rate?
  9. What are the key elements of your website’s design and user experience?
  10. What are the current strengths and weaknesses of your website’s copy, messaging, and calls to action?

By gathering answers to these questions, you can conduct a thorough analysis of the client’s website and identify areas where improvements can be made to increase conversion rates.

User experience (UX) analysis:

To create a user experience (UX) analysis, I would need answers to the following questions:

  1. Who is the target audience or user base?
  2. What is the current user flow or journey within the website or application?
  3. What are the pain points or areas of friction for users within the website or application?
  4. What is the current design and layout of the website or application?
  5. What are the current user engagement metrics, such as bounce rate, time on site, and conversion rate?
  6. Are there any existing user feedback or user testing results available?
  7. Are there any specific features or functionalities that need to be analyzed?
  8. What are the desired outcomes or improvements for the UX analysis?

Conversion funnel analysis

To create a conversion funnel analysis, you will need to ask the client the following questions:

  1. What are the steps that the user needs to take to complete that goal?
  2. How many users start the conversion process, and how many complete it?
  3. At what stage of the funnel do users tend to drop off?
  4. What is the current conversion rate, and what would be an ideal conversion rate?
  5. Are there any technical issues that could be preventing users from completing the funnel?
  6. What are the key pages in the conversion funnel, and how are they currently optimized?
  7. Are there any areas of the funnel that are confusing or unclear to users?
  8. What are the key performance indicators (KPIs) that should be used to measure the success of the funnel?
  9. What are the key improvements that could be made to the funnel to increase conversions?

Landing page optimization

To create a landing page optimization plan, here are some questions that you should consider asking:

  1. What is the goal of the landing page? Is it to sell a product or service, generate leads, or promote a specific offer?
  2. Who is the target audience for the landing page? What are their demographics, interests, and pain points?
  3. What are the key features and benefits of the product or service being promoted on the landing page?
  4. What is the current conversion rate of the landing page? What are the metrics that are being tracked?
  5. What are the strengths and weaknesses of the current landing page design and copy?
  6. Are there any A/B testing or user testing data available for the landing page? If so, what are the insights gained from them?
  7. What are the competitors doing in terms of landing pages and offers?
  8. Are there any technical issues on the landing page that need to be fixed to improve its performance?
  9. What are the best practices and industry standards for landing page design and optimization?
  10. What are the budget and timeline for the landing page optimization project?

A/B testing plan

To create an effective A/B testing plan, here are some questions you may need to ask your client:

  1. What are the goals of your website and what specific actions do you want users to take?
  2. Which pages on your website are currently underperforming and need optimization?
  3. Who is your target audience and what are their preferences and behaviors?
  4. What elements on your website do you want to test (e.g. headlines, images, calls-to-action)?
  5. What variations of those elements do you want to test (e.g. different colors, font sizes, wording)?
  6. What is the expected traffic for the pages being tested and over what period of time?
  7. What software or tools will you use for the testing and analysis?
  8. How will you measure the success of the testing and determine which variation performed better?
  9. How will you implement the changes once the winning variation has been determined?
  10. How frequently will you run A/B tests and how will you determine the priority of the pages to be tested?

Personalization strategy

To create a personalization strategy, you may need to ask the following questions:

  1. What data do you have on your users (e.g., demographics, behavior, preferences, etc.)?
  2. What are the pain points or obstacles that prevent users from converting?
  3. What are the key actions that you want users to take on your website or app?
  4. What are the different segments of your audience that you want to target with personalized experiences?
  5. What types of personalization can you implement (e.g., content, recommendations, offers, etc.)?
  6. How will you measure the success of your personalization efforts?
  7. What tools and technologies will you need to implement your personalization strategy?
  8. How will you continually improve and optimize your personalization efforts over time?

These questions can help you understand your client’s goals, target audience, data, and pain points, and can guide you in developing a personalized experience that is relevant, engaging, and effective in driving conversions.

Retargeting campaigns

To create effective retargeting campaigns, here are some questions you might need to ask your client:

  1. What is the target audience for the retargeting campaign?
  2. What are the specific goals of the retargeting campaign?
  3. What pages or actions on the website should trigger retargeting ads?
  4. What messaging or offer should be used in the retargeting ads?
  5. What platforms should be used for the retargeting ads (Facebook, Google Ads, etc.)?
  6. What budget is allocated for the retargeting campaign?
  7. What are the expected KPIs and how will they be tracked?
  8. How long should the retargeting campaign run?
  9. Are there any special considerations or restrictions for the retargeting campaign (e.g. compliance with advertising guidelines, limited ad inventory, etc.)?

Answering these questions can help you design an effective retargeting campaign that meets your client’s specific needs and goals.
